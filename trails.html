Explore Berry College Trails

Welcome! Berry College offers miles of scenic hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails across its 27,000-acre campus. Trails wind through forests, lakes, and mountains, offering both recreational opportunities and outdoor learning for students.

Research & History

The Berry College trail network is more than just scenic paths. The campus serves as a living outdoor laboratory for ecological studies, land management, and recreation. The iconic Viking Trail connects the main and mountain campuses, while other trails traverse conservation zones and wildlife habitats.

One standout effort is the Berry Longleaf Pine Project, which restores rare longleaf pine stands on Lavender Mountain. Trails like the Longleaf Pine Trail pass through these restoration zones.

Maintenance Notice: The Longleaf Pine Trail is currently under trail maintenance as of November 2025. Please check for updates and stay on designated paths.

Trail Directory

Viking Trail

Distance: ~3 miles | Difficulty: Easy | Surface: Paved

Connects the main campus to the Mountain Campus along a shaded route. Excellent for biking, jogging, and casual walking.

House o’ Dreams Trail

Distance: ~4.8 miles | Difficulty: Moderate | Surface: Natural trail

Leads up Lavender Mountain to the historic House o’ Dreams built in 1922. Offers panoramic views of the campus and surrounding valleys.

Snow Loop

Distance: ~7.2 miles | Difficulty: Moderate to Hard | Surface: Natural trail

Popular loop through forested ridges and open fields. Great for long runs, endurance hikes, and wildlife spotting.

Old Redmond Gap Road Trail

Distance: ~5.4 miles | Difficulty: Moderate | Surface: Old road/natural trail

Follows a quiet old road through pine woods. Ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hurtin’ Gator Trail

Distance: ~3.7 miles | Difficulty: Hard | Surface: Natural trail

Steep climbs and switchbacks provide a challenging trail for hikers and bikers. Scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

Berry Reservoir Trail

Distance: ~2.8 miles | Difficulty: Easy | Surface: Gravel/packed trail

Loop around Berry Reservoir with lake views and abundant wildlife. Great for beginners and casual biking.

Longleaf Pine Trail

Distance: ~3.5 miles | Difficulty: Moderate | Surface: Natural trail

Trail passes through longleaf pine restoration areas. Currently under maintenance as of November 2025.
