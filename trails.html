Explore Berry College Trails

Welcome! Berry College offers miles of scenic hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails across its 27,000-acre campus. Trails wind through forests, lakes, and mountains, offering both recreational opportunities and outdoor learning for students.

Research & History

The Berry College trail network is more than just scenic paths. The campus serves as a living outdoor laboratory for ecological studies, land management, and recreation. The iconic Viking Trail connects the main and mountain campuses, while other trails traverse conservation zones and wildlife habitats.

One standout effort is the Berry Longleaf Pine Project, which restores rare longleaf pine stands on Lavender Mountain. Researchers at Berry traced trees over 200 years old, implemented student-led monitoring, and collaborated with The Nature Conservancy to restore hundreds of acres. Trails like the Longleaf Pine Trail pass through these restoration zones.

Maintenance Notice: The Longleaf Pine Trail is currently under trail maintenance as of November 2025. Please check for route updates and stay on designated paths while restoration is in progress.
